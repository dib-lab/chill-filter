Frequently Asked Questions - chill-filter

Please check out the User Guide!

And, if you have a question that's not answered below, ask it here!)

When I upload my sample, what is being uploaded?!

A k-mer summary (or sketch) of your sample is calculated locally, and then uploaded to the chill-filter Web site. This summary is typically under a megabyte in size, even for large shotgun data sets; you can download it from the chill-filter site if you want to see more.

To calculate the sketch locally, chill-filter uses in-browser k-mer sketching, provided by the branchwater project.

This also means that chill-filter doesn't "see" your reads, or the complete data set; it only has access to the sketch. And you can delete your sketch at any time.

For more information on sketching, as well as an over-abundance of technical details, please see the sourmash documentation.

How can I see which specific genomes are in my sample?

You will be able to, eventually! For now, you'll have follow up on our sequence composition report yourself.

I've downloaded a CSV results file; how do I interpret it?
